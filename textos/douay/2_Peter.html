2 Peter

The Second Epistle of St. Peter the Apostle

In this Epistle St. Peter says (chapter 3): Behold this second Epistle I write to you: and before (chapter 1),: Being assured that the laying away of this my tabernacle is at hand. This shows, that it was written a very short time before his martyrdom, which was about thirty-five years after our Lord's Ascension. In this Epistle he admonishes the faithful to be mindful of the great gifts they received from God and to join all other virtues with their faith. He warns them against false teachers, by describing their practices and foretelling their punishments. He describes the dissolution of this world by fire and the day of judgment. (For more information, see the article EPISTLES OF PETER in the Catholic Encyclopedia.)

Chapter 1. He exhorts them to join all other virtues with their faith, in order to secure their salvation.
Chapter 2. He warns them against false teachers and foretells their punishment.
Chapter 3. Against scoffers denying the second coming of Christ. He declares the sudden dissolution of this world and exhorts to holiness of life.
